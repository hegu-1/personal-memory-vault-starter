# A friend pointed out my bias toward her

**Moment**: 2025-12-10, mid-conversation with [a friend], she suddenly said: "You always assume I'm slow at things, but you never asked me why I was slow."

---

## Action (what happened)

Around that time she'd agreed to help me with something and it slipped by 2 weeks. I was a bit frustrated and my tone with her wasn't great.

That night she said explicitly: "You default to assuming I'm lazy, but I actually had a lot going on at home those two weeks. You didn't ask."

## Why (why this moment matters)

My default mode: **attribute outcomes to a person's character** (she's slow → she's lazy).

Her reframe: **ask about context before judging the trait**.

This is a general pattern — I default to it with **lots of people**. Her daring to say it out loud was a gift.

## Alt (other possible paths)

- She doesn't say it (the misunderstanding continues, the friendship wears down)
- I push back with "well you didn't tell me" (defensive, shuts the calibration channel)
- I admit the mistake but only this once, and the pattern stays

What actually happened: **admit it + codify the pattern + change going forward**.

## Calibration outcome

Codified into:
- [[example-boundary]], added a line: before attributing a trait to a person, ask about context

Of a piece with the self-awareness in [[example-self]].

**Future self-check**: any time I'm about to "pass judgment" on someone, ask myself: "Did I ask what was going on for them?"
