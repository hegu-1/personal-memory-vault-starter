# My view on time management

## Core framing

Time management isn't "cram every minute" — it's "identify which windows your energy is high, and put high-leverage tasks into them."

Time is fixed; energy / attention is the variable. The real leverage is in *matching tasks to your energy state*, not in *squeezing out more time*.

## Three-layer model

1. **Physical layer**: body / sleep / exercise / diet — sets your energy ceiling
2. **Cognitive layer**: deep thinking vs. shallow execution vs. creative divergence — each needs a different mental state
3. **Relational layer**: collaborating with others vs. solo time — each has its own demand

## My operating principles

- 9am–noon = deep-thinking window: no appointments, no meetings
- Afternoon = collaboration / meetings / communication
- Evening = shallow execution (email / tidying up / reading)

For specific techniques see [[example-pomodoro]] (pomodoro) and [[example-weekly-review]] (weekly review).

## Anti-patterns / things I don't do

- Forcing a 6am wake-up (my energy isn't there)
- Multi-threading across tasks (high switching cost)
- Measuring output by to-do list length (measuring the wrong dimension)
